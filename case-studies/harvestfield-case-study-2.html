What's actually at stake

Harvestfield Agro-Processing is the anchor account — a cocoa and palm kernel processor that exports to buyers in China and the Netherlands. The relationship is 7 years deep. 03 — RICE Analysis

Running the numbers

RICE = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) ÷ Effort. Reach = accounts affected. Impact scored 1–3. Confidence as a decimal. Effort in person-weeks.

Where We Override the Model

The loan proposal scores lowest on RICE — high effort, single account. But GTBank and First Bank are already in Segun's office. Every day without a proposal is a day the competition gets more comfortable.

RICE doesn't measure competitive urgency. It doesn't know that a 7-year relationship is one slow response away from walking out the door. So we move the loan proposal to Priority 1 in real-world execution — not because the numbers say so, but because the situation demands it.

This is the difference between using a framework and understanding one.

04 — Priority Order

What Adebimpe does first

The loan proposal and payroll escalation run in parallel — not sequentially. You cannot walk into Segun's office with a ₦220M proposal while his staff are still waiting on delayed salaries.

1
₦220M Loan Proposal — Build and present immediately
GTBank and First Bank are already in the conversation. Speed is the advantage Wema Bank has to manufacture right now. A bespoke proposal structured around Harvestfield's export cycle shows Segun something his competitors cannot — we already know his business.
2
Payroll Escalation — Same day, parallel track
Four delayed payroll runs in five months is not a technical glitch — it's a trust leak. It scores low on Reach but RICE doesn't account for relationship equity. Segun hears about this from his staff every month. Fix it before it becomes the reason he leaves.
3
Export Finance Advisory — Package it with the loan
Deepens the relationship and reduces Harvestfield's FX risk on China and Netherlands contracts. Presented alongside the loan, it turns a financing conversation into a full strategic partnership pitch.
4
Agribusiness Roundtable — Schedule for Q3
Important for portfolio health and sector positioning, but not time-sensitive relative to the other three. Plan it now, execute it once the Harvestfield situation is stabilised.

07 — Panel Q&A

Tough questions, clear answers

The panel is not looking for perfect scores. They are looking for the kind of thinking that makes a great AM — commercially aware, structured, and confident enough to make a call when the picture is not completely clear.

Why did you override RICE for the loan proposal?
RICE measures value versus effort in isolation. It doesn't measure competitive urgency. When two rivals are already in the conversation, speed becomes its own form of impact. The loan proposal's high effort score reflects reality — but the cost of being slow is higher than the cost of the effort itself. We used RICE to structure our thinking, then applied judgement where the model couldn't reach.
Payroll has low Reach — why does it matter so much?
Reach measures how many accounts are affected. Trust doesn't scale that way. One CEO losing confidence because his staff aren't getting paid on time can end a 7-year relationship faster than any competitor can. RICE is not equipped to score relationship equity — that requires the AM's judgement. The payroll issue is a trust leak, and trust leaks don't fix themselves.
What is Wema Bank's single strongest argument against GTBank and First Bank?
They don't know Harvestfield. Adebimpe does. Seven years of transaction history, export cycle data, and relationship capital is not something a competitor can replicate in a pitch meeting. The proposal Wema Bank brings isn't generic — it's structured around the specific rhythms of Harvestfield's cocoa and palm kernel business. That's not a bank selling a loan. That's a partner who has been paying attention.
How do Initiatives 1 and 2 work as a package rather than separate products?
The loan funds the Kaduna expansion. The Export Finance Advisory protects the revenue that expansion generates. One without the other is incomplete. Presented together, they shift the conversation from "bank giving a loan" to "strategic partner invested in Harvestfield's long-term financial health." Segun doesn't buy two products — he gets one coherent vision for his business growth.
What happens if Segun still chooses GTBank or First Bank?
That's a real possibility and a good AM plans for it. If Harvestfield takes the loan elsewhere, the relationship doesn't have to end. Adebimpe maintains the FX trade finance relationship, continues the Export Finance advisory conversation, and keeps showing up. Losing one product doesn't mean losing the account. What matters is that Wema Bank remains the most trusted financial partner in Segun's room — even if not for every product.
